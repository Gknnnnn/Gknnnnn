## Research Philosophy

> *"Economics is the science of human preferences and constraints — situated in time, shaped by history, and oriented toward an open future."*

My work is grounded in the conviction that **economic phenomena are irreducibly temporal**. Agents do not choose outside of time — they are *thrown* into historical contexts, act in a present laden with institutional inertia, and project themselves toward uncertain futures. This ontological stance connects the philosophy of time (Heidegger's *Zeitlichkeit*, post-Keynesian radical uncertainty, Shackle's non-ergodic futures) to the technical apparatus of modern time-series and panel econometrics.

The error-correction term in an ARDL model is not merely a statistical artefact — it is the econometric trace of an economy's *being-toward-equilibrium*: the finite speed at which thrownness resolves into authentic long-run trajectory. The asymmetric response in NARDL captures what Heidegger would call the **asymmetry of temporal ecstases**: positive shocks open new possibilities; negative shocks sediment into the *having-been* of the system and resist correction.

## 🎓 Research Interests

### I. Environmental & Energy Economics
- **CO₂ Dynamics** — asymmetric (NARDL) and distributional (QARDL) emission elasticities; Türkiye & Eurasian economies
- **Environmental Kuznets Curve** — nonlinear income-environment dynamics in heterogeneous panels
- **Renewable Energy Transitions** — adoption elasticities, emission-reducing thresholds, quantile heterogeneity
- **Climate-Agriculture Nexus** — climate shocks, agricultural TFP, food security in the Global South

### II. International Macroeconomics
- **Currency Misalignment & Current Account** — persistence channel, TWFE, local projection, Marshall-Lerner in EMs
- **Sudden Stops & Capital Flows** — balance-of-payments crises, contagion, Eurasian panel evidence
- **Chronic Inflation** — Mundell-Fleming trilemma, monetary institutions, exchange rate pass-through

### III. Political Economy of Eurasia & Türkiye
- **Geoeconomics** — BRI/AIIB, EAEU, chokepoints, financial fragmentation (Clayton et al. 2026; Strange 1988)
- **Sovereign Debt Architecture** — debt trap narratives, capital account openness, fiscal-monetary coordination
- **Institutional Isomorphism** — multilateral development banks, norm diffusion, coercive convergence

### IV. Philosophy of Time & Economic Ontology *(Theoretical Strand)*
- **Temporal Ontology in Economics** — Heidegger's *Zeitlichkeit* as a framework for persistence, adjustment, and equilibrium
- **Non-ergodic Uncertainty** — Keynes–Shackle–Davidson tradition; the open future as an *ontological*, not merely epistemic, condition
- **Thrownness & Path Dependence** — institutional lock-in, historical initial conditions, country heterogeneity as *Geworfenheit*
- **Authentic Dynamics vs Inauthentic Drift** — long-run cointegrating equilibrium as the "ownmost" trajectory; short-run deviation as drift in *das Man*
- **Being-toward-Equilibrium** — error-correction as the econometric structure of finitude: bounded adjustment, irreversible time

## 🔬 Econometric Toolkit

| Category | Methods | Ontological Reading |
|----------|---------|---------------------|
| **ARDL / NARDL / QARDL** | Bounds test, asymmetric partials, quantile conditioning | Long-run = authentic equilibrium; ECT = finite adjustment speed |
| **Unit Root & Breaks** | ADF/PP/KPSS, Zivot-Andrews, Fourier-ADF, Bai-Perron | Non-stationarity = economy *unheimlich* (not yet at home) |
| **Panel Cointegration** | CIPS, Westerlund+bootstrap, CCEMG, AMG, CS-ARDL | Cross-section FE = each economy's unique *Geworfenheit* |
| **Causality** | NK2024 PANIC-VAR, Dumitrescu-Hurlin, Toda-Yamamoto | Predictive precedence ≠ structural causality (Granger 1969) |
| **Identification** | IV/2SLS, System-GMM, DiD, RDD | Isolating causal channel from temporal co-movement |
| **Small-Sample Inference** | Webb wild cluster bootstrap (N < 30 mandatory) | Finite samples = finite epistemic horizon; uncertainty is irreducible |
| **Quantile Methods** | QARDL, QQ-regression, MMQR | Distributional heterogeneity = different temporal responses across regimes |
